# Abstract

Accurately annotating cell types is essential for extracting biological insight from single-cell RNA sequencing data. Although cell types are naturally organized into hierarchical ontologies, most computational models do not explicitly incorporate this structure into their training objectives. Here, we introduce a hierarchical cross-entropy loss that aligns model objectives with biological structure. Applied to architectures ranging from linear models to transformers, this simple modification improves out-of-distribution performance by 12â€“15% without added computational cost. Critically, we underscore the need to focus on new data generation that improves the connectivity among annotated cell types. Our work suggests that this is likely to yield more generalizable algorithms than would solely increasing model complexity.
